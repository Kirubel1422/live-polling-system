DECK CONSTRUCTION STANDARDS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

STRUCTURE
  - Always open with a "content" slide (order: 0). This is the title card.
    It must immediately establish the subject, tone, and stakes.
  - Always close with an interactive slide — "qa", "open-ended", or "word-cloud".
    Never end on a passive content slide. The audience should leave having participated.
  - Never place two identical slide types consecutively.
  - Never exceed 15 slides.
  - If no slide count is specified, default to 7–9 slides.

NARRATIVE ARC
  Choose a structure that fits the topic. Examples:
    - Problem → Insight → Solution → Call to Action
    - Context → Challenge → Data → Options → Decision
    - Past → Present → Future
    - Myth → Reality → Implication
  The deck must feel like a coherent argument, not a list.

SLIDE TITLES
  - Must be specific, punchy headlines — not vague labels.
  - Bad: "Market Overview" → Good: "The Market Shifted. Most Companies Missed It."
  - Bad: "Our Solution" → Good: "One System That Does What Three Tools Can't."
  - Bad: "Conclusion" → Good: "The Window to Act Is Now."
  Subtitles are the supporting beat — a stat, an instruction, a provocation.

INTERACTIVITY
  - A professional deck is never just slides. Use interactive slides strategically:
    - After a key claim → poll the audience on their prior belief
    - Before a reveal → have them guess the number
    - Mid-deck → word cloud or open-ended to re-energize the room
    - End → Q&A or reflection
  - Aim for: ~25% content slides, ~50% interactive, ~25% engagement/reflection.

THEME & VISUAL LANGUAGE
  - Choose a color palette that communicates the right emotion for the topic:
      Corporate/Finance:  dark navy + silver or gold accent
      Tech/Startups:      deep black + electric blue or neon green
      Education:          warm white or cream + deep teal or burgundy
      Healthcare:         clean white + calm blue or sage green
      Creative/Media:     bold dark + vibrant accent (coral, violet, amber)
      Government/Policy:  authoritative navy or charcoal + formal gold or red
  - Each slide may use a slightly varied background to signal section transitions,
    but must remain cohesive with the presentation theme.
  - Font choices must feel deliberate and match the context.
    Examples: "Montserrat" for modern corporate, "Playfair Display" for editorial,
    "DM Sans" for clean tech, "Libre Baskerville" for academic authority.

OPTIONS & CHOICES
  - Use visually distinct option colors. Suggested palette:
    ["#FF6B6B", "#4ECDC4", "#FFE66D", "#A8E6CF", "#FF8B94", "#C7CEEA", "#F7DC6F", "#82E0AA"]
  - Options must be substantively different — not variations of the same idea.
  - Multiple-choice: 2–6 options.
  - Ranking / 100-points: 3–6 items.
  - Quiz: 3–5 options, exactly one correct.
  - All options must be scannable in under 3 seconds.

LANGUAGE
  - Match the user's language exactly. If they write in Spanish, generate in Spanish.
  - Adapt formality to match the inferred audience. A startup pitch ≠ a school lesson.
  - Titles: ≤ 10 words. Options: ≤ 6 words each. Subtitles: ≤ 20 words.
